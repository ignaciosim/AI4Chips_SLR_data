This review follows the Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses (PRISMA) 2020 statement [Page et al. 2021, BMJ 372:n71], adapted to an engineering systematic literature review where the medical-context items on risk-of-bias assessment and certainty-of-evidence scoring are replaced by (i) a peer-review journal-only filter at retrieval and (ii) a high-confidence ontology-match threshold at classification. The complete Scopus query strings (one per lifecycle phase) are defined as source-of-truth in `slr_ontology.py` in the companion code repository (`https://github.com/ignaciosim/AI4Chips_SLR`, commit `e76d2a1`). The retrieval window is 2015–2026 inclusive, restricted to `SRCTYPE(j) AND DOCTYPE(ar)` against a curated venue allow-list (`venues_eda.txt`). Deduplication, ontology-based classification, high-confidence filtering, false-positive correction, and manual curation are each implemented as separate reproducible pipeline stages.

Figure N presents the PRISMA 2020 flow diagram for this review. The retrieval returned 6,835 records across six lifecycle-phase queries. After deduplication (1,304 records removed), 5,531 unique papers entered ontology-based screening, of which 2,435 (44.0%) were classified as AI-for-Chips at some confidence level. Of these, 336 reached the high-confidence threshold (AI method co-occurring with a chip design task, without conflicting hardware-artefact or AI-workload signals). A targeted filter removed 15 generative-adversarial-network false positives caused by the "GAN" / "GaN" lexical collision (i.e., gallium nitride material papers), leaving a 321-paper high-confidence corpus. Manual curation applied three further filters: survey and review papers (4), manually-identified chips-for-AI or off-topic false positives (19), and six stage-reassignments where the automated classifier had misplaced a paper across the lifecycle. The final corpus for synthesis is 298 papers; 53 of these are displayed in the per-stage shortlist tables in the Results section, selected by a blended criterion combining top-cited anchors, method-and-task-diverse exemplars, high-cites-per-year recent papers (2023 onward), 2026 papers surfaced regardless of citation count, and editorial promotions for topical whitespace.

All pipeline outputs — Scopus retrievals per phase, the classified corpus, the high-confidence subset with affiliations and citations, the curated shortlist with per-paper gists, the IEEE references, and the OpenAlex citation-network cache — are available in the companion data repository (`https://github.com/ignaciosim/AI4Chips_SLR_data`). The source of every curation decision (survey removals via keyword, manual false-positive exclusions keyed by DOI, stage overrides, and editorial promotions) is documented in `analysis/generate_stage_shortlist.py` in the code repository. A protocol was not pre-registered on PROSPERO or a comparable platform because PROSPERO does not accept engineering SLRs; methodological decisions were instead frozen into the source code at the time of each pipeline run (commit hashes and data-repository tags document the exact state for each analysis).
